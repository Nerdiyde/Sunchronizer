# Sunchronizer — Prototype Development History

This document archives the physical prototypes built during the development of the Sunchronizer solar tracker.
Each "mark" represents a distinct iteration, progressing from rough first concepts toward the final design.
The timeline spans from the earliest proof-of-concept in early 2024 to the most recent full-featured prototype in early 2026.

> Dates are derived from EXIF metadata of the original photos.

---

## Mark 1 — First Proof of Concept

**Period: March 24, 2024 – July 14, 2024**

The mark 1 was the very first physical prototype of the Sunchronizer — a rough but functional proof of concept built to validate the core mechanics of the tracker system. The focus at this stage was purely on feasibility: could a simple actuator-driven frame be built and controlled to tilt a panel ? The structure was assembled primarily from off-the-shelf hardware and initial 3D-printed brackets, mounted on a 2020/2060 aluminum extrusion profile. Electronics were minimal — just enough to test motion. A major documentation session on April 13, 2024 captured the assembled prototype from multiple angles. The final photo from July 14, 2024 documents the state of mark 1 before transitioning to the next iteration.

## Mark 2 — Refined Mechanics & Dual-Axis Development

**Period: August 2, 2024 – February 22, 2025**

Mark 2 represented a significant step forward. With the core concept confirmed by mark 1, effort shifted toward refining the mechanical structure and exploring dual-axis capabilities. The azimuth rotation mechanism — based on a segmented base ring, geared motor mount, and endstop system — was introduced and iterated during this period. Multiple photo sessions throughout August and October 2024 document the build-up of the improved frame, motor integration, and overall assembly. By February 2025, the mark 2 hardware was mature enough to serve as the reference for the final design language. The `SneakPeak` renders included in this folder are early CAD previews of the design directions explored at this stage.

## Mark 3 — Towards the Final Form Factor

**Period: March 17, 2025 – September 7, 2025**

Mark 3 brought the Sunchronizer closer to its final shape. Having validated the dual-axis mechanism with mark 2, this iteration focused on consolidating the design into a more compact and reliable package. The concrete-plate holder feet, refined actuator mounts, and improved cable routing reflect the shift from prototyping toward a build intended for real outdoor use. An initial photo from March 2025 documents an early-stage assembly, while the main documentation sessions in August and September 2025 capture the nearly complete mark 3 prototype. The September 2025 close-up shots (square format) highlight fine mechanical details of the final assembly.

## Mark 4 — Current Prototype

**Period: February 27–28, 2026**

Mark 4 is the most current hardware prototype of the Sunchronizer, documented just weeks before this history log was created. This iteration incorporates the custom PCB v1.3 (see below) and represents the culmination of roughly two years of iterative mechanical and electrical design. The build was photographed intensively over two consecutive days in late February 2026, with 20 images capturing assembly, component fit, and the overall finished state.

## PCB History — PCB v1.3

**Period: October 5, 2025**

The PCB v1.3 marks the beginning of the custom electronics history for the Sunchronizer. Prior to this, the electronics were built on breadboards or using hand-wired perfboards. PCB v1.3 integrates the ESP32-S3 microcontroller, dual H-bridge motor drivers, BNO085 IMU, DS3231 RTC, and all supporting circuitry onto a single dedicated board — a major milestone toward a reliable and reproducible build. The five photos from October 5, 2025 document the first assembled PCB v1.3 units.
